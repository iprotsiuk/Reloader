# Underworld Progression Design

> **Prerequisites:** Read [../design/core-architecture.md](../design/core-architecture.md), [2026-03-08-reloader-breakout-vision-design.md](2026-03-08-reloader-breakout-vision-design.md), and [2026-03-08-reloader-breakout-vision-implementation-plan.md](2026-03-08-reloader-breakout-vision-implementation-plan.md) first.

---

## Purpose

Define progression that strengthens the criminal-sniper fantasy instead of adding generic leveling.

The player should feel like they are climbing into a nastier underworld tier:
- better jobs
- better prep options
- better intel
- better workshop capability

The reward is new leverage, not abstract XP.

---

## Core Rule

Progression should unlock new ways to solve contracts.

Good progression rewards:
- unlock a new job type
- unlock a new prep option
- unlock better intel before the shot
- unlock a cleaner or riskier solution path

Bad progression rewards:
- flat stat inflation with no visible job payoff
- detached skill trees with no world fiction
- generic "level 2 contracts" gating with no new fantasy

---

## Progression Buckets

| Bucket | What It Should Unlock | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Workshop upgrades | better load development, better consistency, cleaner prep flow | makes the hideout feel like a criminal craft space, not just UI storage |
| Intel access | route notes, exposure windows, vantage hints, witness density warnings | changes how the player plans the shot |
| Ammo and component access | specialty bullets, better powder/component quality, later suppressed/subsonic options | gives the player a reason to care about the round, not just the rifle |
| Higher-tier contracts | nastier constraints, better payout, more expressive shot problems | makes progression visible in mission variety |

These buckets should reinforce each other. Better jobs fund better prep, and better prep unlocks jobs that would otherwise feel unfair.

---

## First Concrete Rewards

### 1. Specialty supplier access

Unlock:
- access to the first specialty ammo path
- contracts where the ammo choice is the real puzzle

Recommended first fantasy:
- AP-capable ammunition for glass or light cover

Why it works:
- very readable in screenshots and trailers
- fits the prep-first pitch
- gives ammo identity beyond quality tiers

### 2. Intel desk / fixer contact

Unlock:
- more precise target timing
- clearer notes about exposure windows or protected zones
- early access to trickier jobs before the player can brute-force them

Why it works:
- turns intel into a real planning surface
- gives the underworld progression a human face
- supports future routine-heavy jobs without requiring full social simulation now

### 3. Precision workshop upgrade

Unlock:
- better consistency tools
- higher-confidence long shots
- premium contracts that assume more disciplined prep

Why it works:
- strengthens the workshop as the emotional home base
- preserves the hardcore-prep identity
- makes reinvestment feel tangible

---

## What This Should Feel Like In Play

Early progression:
- simple jobs
- basic workshop
- limited component options

Mid progression:
- better intel
- first specialty rounds
- contracts with real modifiers

Later progression:
- premium long-range jobs
- nastier constraints
- more deliberate loadout decisions before leaving the hideout

The player fantasy is not "I gained levels."
It is "I now have the contacts, bench, and ammo to take uglier jobs."

---

## Anti-Goals

- Do not add a generic XP bar unless it maps to a real underworld fiction.
- Do not make progression mostly about price inflation and larger numbers.
- Do not unlock new contract tiers without also changing the mission problems.
- Do not let workshop progression become pure decoration with no contract consequence.

---

## Recommendation

Treat underworld progression as capability unlocks tied to job expression.

The first meaningful progression rewards should unlock:
1. a new ammo-driven solution
2. better pre-shot intel
3. a workshop upgrade that supports premium contract confidence

That keeps progression aligned with the breakout vision instead of drifting into a generic economy ladder.

---

## Open Questions

1. Should the first underworld unlock come from a named fixer/contact or from a pure shop upgrade path?
2. How much of workshop progression should be visual immediately versus mechanical first?
3. Should higher-tier contracts require reputation with specific underworld contacts, or only money plus solved jobs?
